3-point perspective in 60 seconds

In a perspective drawing, parallel lines in 3D converge at a vanishing point (VP) on the picture plane. A box aligned to three mutually perpendicular axes gives three VPs — Vx, Vy, Vz — one per axis. The closer a VP sits to the box, the stronger the foreshortening along that axis; pushing a VP to infinity makes that axis orthographic (parallel, no foreshortening).

The triangle formed by the three VPs is the cone of vision: its orthocenter is the camera's principal point, and its interior is the region a physical pinhole could actually photograph. Lines that wander outside this triangle become hyperbolically distorted — the projection no longer corresponds to a real view. The app dashes box edges that exit the triangle to flag this.

The line through any two VPs is a horizon for the third axis. Eye-level horizon = the line through the two horizontal-axis VPs.

What you can drag

  • Box corners — large dots are anchors (c0, c1, c2, c4); small dots are derived from the VPs. The opposite corner (c7) auto-computes.
  • Vanishing points — drag the labeled VP dots directly. The corresponding anchor rotates with the VP.
  • The whole box — hover anywhere on the box and drag with the gizmo. The VPs stay planted; the box re-fits with proper foreshortening.
  • Reference images — same gizmo idea, drag to position.

Toolbox layers

  • Each layer has visibility (●/○), opacity, and (where applicable) a color swatch — click for a hue slider, or use the native picker on the Background layer.
  • Grid layers fan rays from each VP through c0; tweak Angle and 1/dist to rotate the VP around c0 or slide it along its ray. 1/dist at 0 puts the VP at infinity (orthographic on that axis).
  • Horizon layers draw the line through each VP pair, solid between and dashed beyond.
  • VP triangle shows the cone of vision.
  • Wireframe box tints its 1–3 camera-facing faces in the corresponding axis colors.

Navigation & shortcuts

  • Pan — drag empty canvas. Zoom — scroll wheel (zooms into the cursor).
  • Touch — single finger pans / drags handles; two fingers pinch-zoom and pan together.
  • Undo / redo — Cmd/Ctrl+Z, Cmd/Ctrl+Shift+Z (or Cmd/Ctrl+Y).
  • Reset — the button in the toolbox header restores the default box and VPs.

Tips

  • If a VP is pulled close enough to be inside the box, the cube auto-shrinks on that axis rather than turning inside out.
  • Off-canvas VPs are indicated by small colored arrows along the canvas edge pointing at them.
  • The "ray count" slider applies to all three grids — higher values give finer angular spacing around each VP.
